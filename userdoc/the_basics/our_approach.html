Unlike other form processors, Form Tools was designed to be completely separate from the forms themselves.

That's weird. Why?

Forms come in all shapes and sizes, utilizing a variety of technologies - HTML, Ajax, Flash, Silverlight, etc. Different situations require different technologies; different problems require different solutions. So rather than force you to implement your forms in a particular way, our approach was to focus on the form processing instead: our goal was to provide a simple, clear user interface for managing the form information.

There are many excellent form builders out there - some desktop applications, some web-based. All of them generate forms in different ways, and depending on your needs, one may be a better fit than another. That's when Form Tools steps in: it doesn't care WHERE the form came from: it can be used with any of them.

Having said all that...

Having said all that, Form Tools 2.1.0 does actually let you generate forms within the interface, and, with the aid of the premium Form Builder module, publish them directly on your site. The Form Builder simply provides you with another means to creating your forms: it is not required, and depending on your particular scenario, you may prefer to build your own forms.
